# EDM-Methodology

This repository is maintained by Kyle Walter. It serves as the central place where I publish my methodology for airdrop legitimacy research, including the framework outlined in my document The EquiDrop Maestros. Here you will find detailed explanations of how I assess projects, the prompts I use in my research process, updates to my methodology as the space evolves, and notes on how my service process is structured. Future technical details will also be added here as they become relevant.

The purpose of this repository is not only to share my work but also to encourage others to walk around the repo, explore the files, and understand the reasoning behind each step. I want this to be a resource that helps crypto enthusiasts develop their own mid-to-professional-level auditing skills, so they can conduct independent research with confidence.

What you can expect here
- methodology documents explaining the five-pillar legitimacy assessment
- prompts used in my AI-assisted research workflow
- updates to scoring and monitoring processes
- transparency notes about sourcing and independence
- technical details as they are developed
- service process documentation for community engagement

About me
I am Kyle Walter, founder and lead researcher of The EquiDrop Maestros. My mission is to prioritize legitimacy over hype and empower participants to make informed decisions in the airdrop space. I operate independently, without paid promotions or financial conflicts of interest, and I publish my findings with full transparency.
